Youth Friendly DevConf.CZ 2019

For the first time in the history of DevConf, the upcoming 11th free open source conference DevConf.CZ 2019 invites young speakers under age of 19 to:

  • give them an opportunity to share their ideas, projects and show their talent in open source
  • demonstrate that FOSS technologies are there for everyone in every age
  • promote FOSS technologies usable anywhere from kindergartens to universities

DevConf.CZ organizers see an ideal speaker as an young student, that have a certain interest and experience in open source technologies, with good English [1], willing to present at DevConf.CZ in front of a mixed audience, i.e. adult visitors and young guests.

Each young speaker can choose a time duration of their talk or workshop (5, 10, 20, 30 min) Talks or workshops can be solo and collaborative.

The CfP deadline for young speakers differs from other submissions and is extended to November 2. The accepted speakers will be announced on November 12.

All DevConf.CZ participants younger than 17 must be accompanied by a parent or guardian.

[1] DevConf.CZ conference is in English. The organizers can make some exceptions for Czech only speakers, with suggestions to make slides in English.

Important dates

  • CfP closes: November 2, 2018
  • Accepted speakers confirmation: November 19, 2018
  • Schedule published: December 10, 2018
  • Event dates: Friday, January 25 to Sunday, January 27, 2019

DevConf Contributor Conferences are technology conference events for Linux and JBoss Developers, Admins, Linux and other open source project contributors, organized by Red Hat.

Our aim in organizing the conference is to provide opportunities for developers and contributors to meet and discuss future development of upstream projects and share updates and announcements about recent progress.

Red Hat is the leading industry provider of open-source software and operating systems. Red Hat's open-source software provides flexible alternatives to proprietary operating systems such as UNIX. Renowned within the industry, Red Hat employs more than 10,000 employees and maintains operations in 60 offices worldwide. More at redhat.com.
